Firefox is based on an open source project, what does it mean? It means there is no initial developer, it is written by a community of programmers from the entire world. The support may be limited but you will get a lot of help through the forums and mailing lists. Open source projects don't focus to benefit individuals, while it is for the benefit of the community. Firefox is one of the most used freeware in the history of software. 

But how does Firefox make money?

Firefox is supported by Mozilla Corporation. It was started as a non-profit organization but now it is not at all a non-profit organization. Yes! it earns a lot of money. Google is the main source of money for Firefox. Since Firefox 3.0 is launched it was packed with a default search engine in it "Google."  Google is the default search engine as well as the default start page in Firefox browser and Google pays a hefty amount on this contract for about 80 to 85 percent of the revenue earned through this.

Firefox also earns a lot of money through the Mozilla Store where we can buy tee shirts, caps, bags, pins etc. Firefox sells all these items at a huge price and lot of people buy these because people love using such accessories from such great brands. And ya lots of people and different organizations donate Firefox for such wonderful work they do.

Google for Mitchell Baker's (Chair person of Mozilla Foundation) videos on Firefox, you will get to know lots of interesting things about Firefox.
